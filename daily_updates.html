Daily Updates

November 7, 2017

  • For our first rehearsal, we started out with a company meeting! I got to meet the cast and I am gradually learning everyone's names. We went over general rules and received our music scores. After that, we had our first music rehearsal in which we learned almost all of the music for the show that involves everyone.

    From there, the Cratchit family split off to work on the songs that pertain only to the seven of us. We stopped for lunch a hair early, which gave the three of us (Mac aka Francis, Sasha aka Tiny Tim, and myself) time to do some homework before heading off to a favorite tradition of ours: the annual chili feed!

    Each year, the staff at the Omaha Community Playhouse makes several Crock Pots full of chili to feed everyone in the building, and there are veggies and desserts and cornbread and Fritos. I'm getting hungry just thinking about it!

    Following the massive amounts of chili, everyone met back up to have a dialect session with one of our directors, Jeff Horger. Fun Fact- did you know that the difference between a dialect and an accent are that a dialect is in the actual language and refers to how you speak that language whereas an accent is how your native language affects the language you are speaking in (which is most likely a second or third language)? I didn't know that.

    Next, we talked through the Cratchit scenes. We discussed the meaning behind some of the lines, and how certain things can be inferred about the family based on the way things are worded in the text.

    From 4:00 - 4:30, the whole cast was back in the rehearsal hall learning the last bit of music from the entire show. We sang the closing number to close our first day of rehearsals.

November 8, 2017

  • Today was a pretty easy going day. We just had an hour of rehearsal that started at 4 (so I didn't miss any school), and we just visited over some Cratchit things, including our Christmas Yet To Come scenes and our Christmas Present scenes.

November 9, 2017

  • To start off rehearsals today, we began with a scene from Christmas Past where Scrooge sees his sister and friends singing carols together before Christmas. Between working on Chrictmas Past and our next scene, Sasha Mac and I filled out our Secret Scrooge forms! Secret Scrooge is an optional game of Secret Santa that the cast plays every year, and this year our chaperone Brittany is getting in on the fun and playing, too! I had enough down time where I could go back up to Marian for the last few classes of the day, and then I was able to make it back to the theater in time for our third Cratchit family meeting. Throughout reviewing scenes we've finished blocking and learning the the new ones, the 7 of us Cratchits decided that we are going to create a special family handshake. We haven't started putting it together yet, but I will be sure to post about it when we do!

November 10, 2017

  • Today started off with a block of time where I could go to school and take a handful of tests. Rehearsal didn't start until 2:00, so I was able to get a lot of my school work done AT school :). When we did start rehearsal, we jumped straight into one of my favorite scenes: Counting House! the Counting House scene is where Scrooge is trying to work peacefully in his shop when these kids come in and ask for money. The kids know that he hates children, and the scream and run around the shop as he chases them threatening to hit them with a yard stick.

November 11, 2017

  • Street Scene Day! Today is the day in which we learn all of the Street Scenes! The Street Scenes are the scenes in which everyone is on stage at once, and each person has a set path that they have to follow. These paths have been perfected so that no one is hit by any moving scenery or run into by anyone else. This is one of my favorite days in the rehearsal period.

November 13, 2017

  • After having Sunday, November 12, off, we officially move into Omaha South High School for rehearsals. From now until the end of the rehearsal process, we will be at South with full set as we gradually add in props and costumes. Today, all we did was make sure that set pieces were spiked correctly and that there was enough romfor the scene to happen within the given space. I will try to start adding more pictures now that we have more time between scenes!

Brittany (the best chaperone ever) let us walk to Jacobo's ( a little grocery store down the street from the theater) so we could pick up some chips and salsa to go with our lunches. After I was defeated in two card games in a row, we jumped into our first full run through of the show!
    Rehearsal ended at about 5pm, which left just enough time to get dinner before the cast went to see the Mainstage version of A CHRISTMAS CAROL perform at OCP! Overall, it was a wonderful day.
